20 Incredible Football Facts

← Back to Home

1. Origin: Football was originally invented in China around 476 BC, where it was called 'Cuju' and played with a leather ball stuffed with feathers.
2. Historic Scoring: The highest-scoring football game in history took place in 2002, ending in a staggering 149-0 scoreline between AS Adema and SO l'Emyrne.
3. Production Capital: More than 80% of the world's professional footballs are manufactured in a single city: Sialkot, located in Pakistan.
4. Climate Limits: Greenland cannot officially join FIFA because the extreme arctic climate prevents them from growing sustainable grass pitches.
5. Lightning Speed: The fastest goal in professional football history was scored by Gavin Stokes in just 2.1 seconds straight from the kickoff whistle.
6. Perfect Grid: Cristiano Ronaldo has achieved the near-impossible feat of scoring a competitive goal in every single minute layout from 1 to 90.
7. Solid Gold Trophy: The official FIFA World Cup trophy is crafted using solid 18-karat gold and weighs approximately 6.1 kilograms.
8. Television Debut: The first official live TV broadcast of a football match took place in 1937, featuring a friendly between Arsenal and their reserve squad.
9. Endurance Athletics: An average professional outfield football player runs between 10 to 13 kilometers throughout a single standard 90-minute match.
10. Nature Strike: In 1998, a freak lightning strike tragically killed an entire 11-man football team in the DRC, while the opposing team walked away unharmed.
11. The King's Record: The legendary Pelé holds the unbroken world record for winning the most FIFA World Cup winner medals, capturing 3 career titles.
12. First International: The first official international football fixture in history was played all the way back in 1872 between traditional rivals Scotland and England.
13. Colossal Arena: The largest operational stadium in the world is the Rungrado 1st of May Stadium, located in Pyongyang, North Korea, holding over 114,000 spectators.
14. Timeless Design: The overall sphere shape and dimensional proportions of the modern football have remained practically unchanged for over 120 historic years.
15. Golden Streak: Lionel Messi secured a historic world record by winning 4 consecutive Ballon d'Or awards between the years 2009 and 2012.
16. Modern Era Break: The English Premier League (EPL) as we know it today was officially founded and separated from the old Football League system in 1992.
17. Early Entry: The youngest professional football debut was made by Mauricio Baldivieso, who entered a top-flight Bolivian league match at just 12 years old.
18. Flawless Strategy: The iconic Zinedine Zidane was notoriously never once caught in an offside position throughout his entire UEFA Champions League career.
19. Inaugural Tournament: The first FIFA World Cup tournament ever held was hosted and won by Uruguay in 1930, featuring just 13 invitational nations.
20. Wild Delay: A top-tier club match in the Norwegian league was once officially delayed because a massive swarm of aggressive bees occupied one of the goalposts.
