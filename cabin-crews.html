Cabin Crew Secrets

← Back Hub
1. Taste Alteration: Engine noise combined with high cabin dryness decreases a passenger's tastebud sensitivity by up to 30%.
2. Intense Training: Flight attendants undergo extreme survival training, including self-defense, fighting cabin fires, and birth delivery.
3. Strict Height Benchmarks: Cabin crew height regulations are strictly optimized so they can reach essential ceiling safety bins and overhead locks.
4. Hidden Bedrooms: Long-haul jumbo jets (Boeing 777/787) have hidden overhead bunks above passenger seats for crew sleep shifts.
5. Lock Control: Flight attendants can easily bypass and unlock airplane lavatory doors from the outside using a hidden latch under the 'Lavatory' sign.
6. Secret Flight Codes: Crew members use subtle codes like 'Tom' or 'Jim' to handle difficult passengers or medical issues without causing cabin panic.
7. Glassware Dangers: Flight attendants prefer serving canned beverages directly rather than pouring because of the sanitary risks of ice scoops.
8. Medical Power: Cabin crew have direct satellite-phone access to emergency physicians on the ground to manage serious medical crises mid-flight.
9. No Pay For Boarding: In most airlines, cabin crew are only paid for 'block time'—meaning they aren't paid during passenger boarding.
10. Authority Powers: Flight attendants are sworn safety officers, and ignoring their direct instructions is a punishable federal crime.
