20 Flight & Cabin Crew Facts

← Back to Home

1. Cabin pressure is artificially maintained because high-altitude air is not breathable for humans.
2. Air inside aircraft cabins is extremely dry, often lower humidity than deserts.
3. Cabin crew are trained in emergency medical response including CPR and first aid.
4. Emergency oxygen masks provide only about 10–15 minutes of oxygen supply.
5. Flight attendants undergo survival training for fire, evacuation, and water landings.
6. Aircraft are designed to handle turbulence far stronger than what passengers usually experience.
7. Cabin lighting is dimmed during flights to help passengers adjust to time zones.
8. Cabin crew must demonstrate full aircraft evacuation within 90 seconds during certification.
9. Cabin crew use silent hand signals and coded phrases for communication onboard.
10. Pilots and cabin crew eat different meals to reduce the risk of simultaneous food poisoning.
11. Pilots follow a “sterile cockpit” rule below 10,000 feet to avoid distractions.
12. Aircraft are struck by lightning occasionally but are built to safely withstand it.
13. Cabin crew jump seats are reinforced for impact safety during takeoff and landing.
14. Jet lag happens due to disruption of the body’s internal circadian rhythm.
15. Cabin crew training programs can last from several weeks to months.
16. Airplane toilets use vacuum systems instead of traditional water flushing systems.
17. Airlines enforce strict grooming and uniform standards for cabin crew.
18. Aircraft windows are made of multiple layers of acrylic for pressure safety.
19. Cabin crew are trained to operate emergency exits and door mechanisms.
20. Commercial aviation is one of the safest forms of long-distance travel.
