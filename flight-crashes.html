Flight Crashes & Safety

← Back Hub
1. Black Box Birth: Australian scientist David Warren invented the flight data and cockpit voice recorder in 1953 after investigating a Comet jet crash.
2. Tenerife Disaster: The deadliest crash in aviation history occurred in 1977 when two Boeing 747s collided on a foggy runway in Tenerife, claiming 583 lives.
3. CRM Birth: Crew Resource Management (CRM) was introduced in 1979 to improve cockpit communication and prevent accidents caused by captain authority intimidation.
4. Wind Shear Shields: Microburst wind shears caused major crashes in the 1980s, leading to the development of Terminal Doppler Weather Radar (TDWR) systems.
5. GPWS Integration: Ground Proximity Warning Systems (GPWS) have virtually eliminated controlled flight into terrain (CFIT) accidents since becoming mandatory.
6. TCAS Revolution: Traffic Collision Avoidance Systems (TCAS) communicate directly between airplanes to coordinate visual and auditory instructions to avoid mid-air collisions.
7. Hudson River Miracle: US Airways Flight 1549 proved passenger cabin prep was flawless when pilots ditched a bird-struck Airbus A320 safely into the Hudson River.
8. Flier Fireproofing: Modern airplanes utilize composite, fire-resistant cabin materials and emergency lighting tracks that show exit paths through thick smoke.
9. The 90-Second Rule: Every commercial aircraft must pass stringent evacuation tests, proving all passengers can escape within 90 seconds using only half the exits.
10. Unbelievable Odds: Statistics show that a passenger would need to take a commercial flight every day for 22,000 years to have a 100% chance of experiencing a crash.
