History of Aviation

← Back Hub
1. Ancient Dreams: Abbas Ibn Firnas, a 9th-century Andalusian polymath, made the first recorded scientific glider flight attempt in Spain using feathers.
2. Leonardo's Concept: In 1485, Leonardo da Vinci designed his famous Ornithopter drawings, taking inspiration directly from the wing structure of bats.
3. Hot Air Pioneers: The Montgolfier brothers launched the first successful manned hot air balloon flight in Paris in 1783, carrying a sheep, a duck, and a rooster.
4. Father of Gliders: Sir George Cayley discovered the four fundamental aerodynamic forces of flight in 1799: Lift, Drag, Thrust, and Weight.
5. The Breakthrough: Orville and Wilbur Wright achieved the first sustained, controlled, powered airplane flight on December 17, 1903, at Kitty Hawk, North Carolina.
6. English Channel: Louis Blériot became the first person to fly a monoplane across the English Channel in 1909, proving aviation was a viable cross-border transport.
7. World War I Jumps: The demands of WWI accelerated aviation technology rapidly, transitioning biplanes from fragile wood and fabric to durable steel-framed models.
8. Metal Monocoque: Hugo Junkers built the world's first all-metal cantilever aircraft, the Junkers J 1, in Germany in 1915.
9. First Flight Attendant: Heinrich Kubis was appointed as the world's first flight attendant on German DELAG passenger airships in 1912.
10. Golden Era Start: The post-WWI era birthed civil commercial aviation, converting wartime bombers into early mail carriers and primitive passenger cabins.
