20th Century Aviation

← Back Hub
1. The Atlantic Leap: Charles Lindbergh completed the first non-stop solo transatlantic flight in 1927, flying the 'Spirit of St. Louis' from NY to Paris.
2. Jet Engine Era: Sir Frank Whittle of Great Britain and Hans von Ohain of Germany independently developed the turbojet engine in the late 1930s.
3. Sound Barrier Broken: On October 14, 1947, Chuck Yeager flew the experimental Bell X-1 rocket plane past Mach 1, breaking the sound barrier.
4. The Jetliner Arrival: The de Havilland Comet became the world's first commercial passenger jetliner when it entered active service in 1952.
5. Jumbo Jet Dawn: The maiden flight of the iconic Boeing 747 in 1969 completely democratized international travel by dramatically lowering cost-per-seat.
6. Supersonic Concorde: Concorde began commercial supersonic flights in 1976, allowing travelers to fly from London to New York in under 3.5 hours.
7. World's Largest Airplane: In 1988, the Antonov An-225 Mriya made its first flight, specifically engineered to transport Soviet space shuttles.
8. Blackbird Supremacy: The Lockheed SR-71 Blackbird was deployed in 1966, flying at altitudes over 85,000 feet and speeds surpassing Mach 3.
9. Radar Invisibility: The F-117 Nighthawk stealth attack aircraft entered operational military status in 1983, utilizing complex faceting angles to deflect radar.
10. Fly-By-Wire Shift: The launch of the Airbus A320 in 1987 introduced complete digital fly-by-wire flight control systems into civil airliners.
