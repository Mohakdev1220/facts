🚀 Amazing Space Facts 🌌

← Back to Home

1. Space is completely silent because there is no air to carry sound.
2. Venus is the hottest planet in our solar system, even hotter than Mercury.
3. A day on Venus is longer than a year on Venus.
4. Neutron stars are so dense that even a tiny amount would weigh an enormous amount on Earth.
5. The Sun contains almost all of the mass in our solar system.
6. There are more stars in the universe than grains of sand on all Earth's beaches.
7. Space is not completely empty; it contains dust, gas, and dark matter.
8. Saturn is less dense than water, so it would float if there were a giant enough ocean.
9. Black holes are invisible because their gravity is so strong that even light cannot escape.
10. Moon footprints can remain for millions of years because there is no wind or water to erase them.
11. Jupiter has the shortest day of all the planets, lasting about 10 hours.
12. Olympus Mons on Mars is the largest volcano in the solar system.
13. The Milky Way is on a collision course with the Andromeda galaxy.
14. Uranus rotates on its side, unlike most other planets.
15. Jupiter’s Great Red Spot is a giant storm that has lasted for centuries.
16. 55 Cancri e is an exoplanet that may have carbon-rich material inside it.
17. The first woman in space was Valentina Tereshkova in 1963.
18. About 1 million Earths could fit inside the Sun.
19. Pluto is now classified as a dwarf planet.
20. Astronauts on the International Space Station see many sunrises and sunsets every day.
