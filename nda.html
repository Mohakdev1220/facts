20 NDA Facts

← Back to Home

1. The National Defence Academy (NDA) is the joint services academy of the Indian Armed Forces, where cadets of the Army, Navy, and Air Force train together before going to their respective pre-commission training academies.
2. Located in Khadakwasla near Pune, Maharashtra, the massive academy campus sprawls over 7,015 acres of scenic, mountainous terrain.
3. NDA holds the proud distinction of being the world's first tri-service academy, pioneering the concept of joint military training.
4. The legendary motto of the NDA is "Seva Paramo Dharma" (Service Before Self), which was formally adopted in 1951 to replace the original "Service Before Self".
5. The main administrative hub of the campus, the majestic Sudan Block, is named in honor of the sacrifices made by Indian troops in the East African Campaign during World War II.
6. The construction of the Sudan Block was funded by a generous £100,000 gift from the Sudanese government in 1941 to thank Indian soldiers for defending their borders.
7. Cadets who graduate from the NDA receive a Bachelor's degree (B.A., B.Sc., or B.Tech) awarded by the prestigious Jawaharlal Nehru University (JNU), New Delhi.
8. Although the first course started in Clement Town, Dehradun in 1949, the Khadakwasla campus was formally inaugurated on January 16, 1955.
9. The ultimate honor for an NDA graduate is the President's Gold Medal, which is awarded to the best overall cadet of the passing-out course.
10. All NDA cadets are systematically organized into 18 Squadrons, which are distributed across 5 Battalions. Each squadron boasts its own distinct flag and proud traditions.
11. To pay tribute to the ultimate sacrifice, the cadets constructed the "Hut of Remembrance" to honor academy alumni who laid down their lives in the line of duty.
12. The iconic Passing Out Parade (POP) of the academy is held at the Khetarpal Parade Ground, named after 2nd Lt. Arun Khetarpal, who was posthumously awarded the Param Vir Chakra.
13. The gorgeous Peacock Bay on the Khadakwasla Lake serves as the primary training ground for Naval cadets, where they learn seamanship, sailing, and water navigation.
14. Air Force cadets at the NDA undergo specialized glider training on the academy's Super Dimona gliders before they ever fly powered aircraft.
15. The rigorous physical training regimen at the academy includes cross-country runs, equestrian training (horse riding), survival camps, and intensive drill routines.
16. The final camp for sixth-term cadets, known as "Camp Greenhorn", is a legendary rite of passage that tests physical endurance, navigation, and survival under highly simulated battle scenarios.
17. Following a historic Supreme Court ruling, the NDA officially welcomed its first batch of female cadets in 2022, marking a major milestone in Indian military history.
18. Alumni of the NDA have led India in every major conflict since independence and include three Param Vir Chakra awardees and dozens of Ashoka Chakra recipients.
19. The NDA has produced multiple Chiefs of Army Staff, Chiefs of Naval Staff, and Chiefs of Air Staff, occasionally leading all three service branches of the military simultaneously.
20. The academy’s training builds deep lifelong bonds across service branches, ensuring that Army, Navy, and Air Force commanders can coordinate seamlessly in times of national crisis.
