20 Inventions Facts

← Back to Home

1. The wheel was invented around 3500 BC in Mesopotamia — but was initially used for pottery, not transportation.
2. Penicillin was discovered accidentally by Alexander Fleming in 1928 when mold contaminated one of his petri dishes.
3. The World Wide Web was invented by Tim Berners-Lee in 1989 as a way for scientists to share information across computers.
4. Thomas Edison held 1,093 U.S. patents, more than any inventor in American history up to his time.
5. Velcro was invented in 1941 by Swiss engineer George de Mestral after he noticed how burr seeds stuck to his dog's fur.
6. The printing press invented by Gutenberg around 1440 enabled mass production of books and helped spark the Renaissance and Reformation.
7. X-rays were discovered by Wilhelm Röntgen in 1895 — he took the first X-ray image of his wife's hand, showing her bones and ring.
8. The steam engine, refined by James Watt in the 1760s, powered the Industrial Revolution and transformed manufacturing and transport.
9. Coca-Cola was originally invented by a pharmacist in 1886 and was marketed as a medicinal tonic before becoming a global beverage.
10. GPS was originally developed by the U.S. military and only became available for civilian use after the downing of Korean Air Lines Flight 007 in 1983.
11. The transistor, invented in 1947 at Bell Labs, replaced vacuum tubes and made modern computers, phones, and electronics possible.
12. Post-it Notes were invented by accident when a researcher at 3M was trying to develop a strong adhesive but created a weak, repositionable one instead.
13. The microwave oven was invented after engineer Percy Spencer noticed his chocolate bar melted while standing near a radar magnetron.
14. Gunpowder was invented by Chinese alchemists in the 9th century while they were searching for an elixir of immortality.
15. The telephone, patented by Alexander Graham Bell in 1876, beat Elisha Gray's similar patent application by just a matter of hours.
16. Safety glass was discovered accidentally in 1903 when a French chemist dropped a flask coated in cellulose nitrate and saw it crack without shattering.
17. The battery was invented by Alessandro Volta in 1800 after observing that certain metals produced electricity when in contact with a moist conductor.
18. Kevlar, the material used in bulletproof vests, was invented by chemist Stephanie Kwolek at DuPont in 1965 while researching lightweight fibers.
19. The internet began as ARPANET in 1969, a U.S. Defense Department network designed to maintain communications in the event of a nuclear attack.
20. CRISPR gene editing, developed in the 2010s, allows scientists to precisely modify DNA sequences and has opened new frontiers in medicine and biology.
